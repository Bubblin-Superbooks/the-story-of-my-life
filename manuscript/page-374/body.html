To Sir John Everett Millais

Perkins Institution for the Blind, South Boston, Mass., April 30, 1891.

It is very beautiful to think that people far away in England feel sorry for a little helpless child in America. I used to think, when I read in my books about your great city, that when I visited it the people would be strangers to me, but now I feel differently.
