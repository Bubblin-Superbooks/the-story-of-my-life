Perhaps an explanation of the method that was in use when I took my examinations will not be amiss here. The student was required to pass in sixteen hoursâ€”twelve hours being called elementary and four advanced. He had to pass five hours at a time to have them counted. The examination papers were given out at nine o'clock at Harvard and brought to Radcliffe by a special messenger. Each candidate was known, not by his name, but by a number. I was No. 233, but, as I had to use a typewriter, my identity could not be concealed.
